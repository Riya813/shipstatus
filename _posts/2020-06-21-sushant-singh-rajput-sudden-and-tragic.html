ALL YOU NEED TO KNOW ABOUT SUSHANT SINGH RAJPUT.

Sushant Singh Rajput's was an Indian Actor. Rajput was born in Patna to Krishna Kumar Singh and Usha Singh. His ancestral home is in Bihar's Purnia district.
Rajput attended St. Karen's High School in Patna and Kulachi Hansraj Model School in New Delhi. According to Rajput, he had ranked seventh in the DCE Entrance Exam in 2003, and secured admission in the Bachelor of Engineering (Mechanical Engineering) class in Delhi College of Engineering. He was also a National Olympiad Winner in Physics. In all, he cleared as many as 11 engineering entrance exams, including that for the Indian School of Mines.

While a student at Delhi Technological University, Rajput enrolled in Shiamak Davar's dance classes. It was only later that the idea of making a career in acting came to him, as some of his fellow students in the dance classes happened to be interested in acting and were attending Barry John's drama classes.

Rajput started his career with television serials. His debut show was Star Plus's romantic drama Kis Desh Mein Hai Meraa Dil (2008), followed by a starring role in Zee TV's popular soap opera Pavitra Rishta (2009–11).

Rajput made his film debut in the buddy drama Kai Po Che! (2013), for which he received a nomination for the Filmfare Award for Best Male Debut. He then starred in the romantic comedy Shuddh Desi Romance (2013) and as the titular detective in the action thriller Detective Byomkesh Bakshy! (2015). His highest-grossing releases came with a supporting role in the satire PK (2014), followed by the sports biopic M.S. Dhoni: The Untold Story (2016). For his performance in the latter, he received his first nomination for the Filmfare Award for Best Actor. Rajput went on to star in the commercially successful films Kedarnath (2018) and Chhichhore (2019).

SUDDEN DEMISE OF SUSHANT SINGH RAJPUT!


The sudden and tragic demise of the 34-year-old, extremely talented actor, Sushant Singh Rajput had sent a wave of shock amongst all. It was reported that on 14th June, Sunday, in his own Bandra (West) apartment, the actor hanged himself to death. He had reportedly been suffering from depression for about six months. No suicide note has been found. According to Mumbai Police, medical prescriptions and medical reports were found in Sushant's room and an investigation is underway. Actor Sushant Singh Rajput’s death has shocked the entire nation. Tributes are pouring in for him and social media is flooded with pictures of the late star. 

Sushant Singh Rajput’s sudden demise leaves everyone question about prejudices in Indian film industry. The biggest TV to film success story has sparked controversy whether TV actors are wholeheartedly accepted in the film industry or there still are prejudices that exist when it comes to TV stars?


CONTROVERSIES SURROUNDING SUSHANT SINGH RAJPUT'S DEMISE.


1. Sushant battled depression and had stopped taking medicine


     Sushant Singh Rajput’s death by suicide led to reports suggesting that the actor had         been facing depression since a few months and the cops had reportedly recovered             anti-depressant pills from his Bandra residence. According to a report on Times               Now,the ‘Kai Po Che’ actor’s sister and his manager had revealed to the Mumbai               Police that Sushant was battling depression. According to the Mumbai Police, he had       stopped taking medicines for it. Sushant’s father had reportedly said in his                         statement  that Sushant often felt low but he wasn’t aware that his son was in                   depression and was being treated for the same.

2. Sushant Singh Rajput was rumoured to get married in                   November

      Sushant Singh Rajput and Rhea Chakraborty were rumoured to be a relationship              since a long time. According to a report on a news portal, Sushant’s cousin had                  stated that the actor was planning to get married, which would be a private affair,            but his bride’s name was not revealed. His family was reportedly gearing up for                wedding preps and was to visit him once the lockdown was relaxed.

3. Family, friends being interrogated

      Several people close to Sushant Singh Rajput were being called by the Mumbai                  police to record their statements. The police have reportedly recorded statements of        10 people including the actor’s family members, his doctor, manager, house help              and friends. Mukesh Chhabra, director of Sushant’s last unreleased film ‘Dil                      Bechara’ had recorded his statement and shared that he knew the 'Raabta' actor                professionally and that the last time they spoke was when Sushant had called him            on his birthday. The last call made by Sushant was to his friend Mahesh Shetty and          his statement was also recorded by the police who are investigating the actor’s                  demise.
4. Shekhar Kapur, Rohini Iyer slam Bollywood

      Sushant Singh Rajput was to collaborate with Shekhar Kapur for ‘Paani’, but the              film was shelved. Mourning Sushant’s demise, the filmmaker had posted on Twitter,        “I knew the pain you were going through. I knew the story of the people that let you        down so bad that you would weep on my shoulder. I wish I was around the last 6              months. I wish you had reached out to me. What happened to you was their Karma.          Not yours.” Netizens urged Shekhar Kapur to name the people he was hinting in the        post. On the other hand, Sushant’s close friend Rohini Iyer had also slammed all              those posting about the actor’s untimely demise and shared, “He didn’t care about            these people who are busy posting about not being in touch with him. For the                    record, he didn’t care about being in touch. He hated fake friends, phone calls and            small talk. He rejected your parties, you didn’t shun him. He rejected your lobbies.          He didn’t need camps, he had his own kingdom. He was a fighter.”
   
      
CASE AGAINST BOLLYWOOD CELEBRITIES
 

A few days after Sushant Singh Rajput's demise, advocate Sudhir Kumar Ojha filed
a criminal complaint Salman Khan and producer-director Karan Johar, Aditya Chopra, Sajid Nadiadwala, Sanjay Leela Bhansali, Bhushan Kumar, Ekta Kapoor, and director Dinesh. According to IANS the complainant had claimed that the celebs did not let Sushant's movie get released under a conspiracy. The lawyer had also alleged that Sushant was not invited to film functions because of these people.

Ekta Kapoor had reacted to the reports stating "Thankyou for the case for not casting... when Actually I LAUCHED HIM. I'm beyond upset at how convoluted theories can be!"

While actor Kangana Ranaut has expressed anger in the matter, saying, “Many things have come out after the murder of Sushant Singh Rajput.” “Sushant faced emotional, psychological, and mental lynching,” she added. “Movie mafias’ not only banned him, but his mind has been broken by stuttering,” the Queen actor said.

The incident also prompted famous singer Sonu Nigam to open up about the “cruel” machinations of the music industry. “Sushant Singh Rajput died, an actor died. Tomorrow, you can hear the same about a singer, or a composer, or a lyrics writer. Because the music scenario in our country… there are bigger ‘mafias’ here that in the film industry, unfortunately,” said he in his vlog.
